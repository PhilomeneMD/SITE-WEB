About the Project

This project aims to build an automated system that monitors catalog acquisitions, publishing signings and record deals worldwide. The Sacem needs accurate and up-to-date information to correctly distribute royalties for both its own members (e.g. Maître Gims) and its mandates – major publishers and labels such as Universal Music Publishing, Sony, Warner Chappell, BMG, Kobalt, Concord, etc.

When a publisher forgets to notify Sacem of a new signing (e.g. Taylor Swift joins Universal), the risk is miscalculation: millions in royalties might be missing from the annual digital payout (YouTube, Spotify, Apple Music...). If these errors accumulate, mandates might leave Sacem for other collection societies like PRS (UK) or GEMA (Germany), costing millions in admin fees.

Our solution is to automate this monitoring with web scraping from trusted sources like Music Business Worldwide, Complete Music Update, Music Ally, Billboard Pro, Rolling Stone, and Digital Music News. This will provide Sacem’s data team with a near real-time view of industry news and help catch missing signings early.

How it Works

1. The system regularly scrapes selected websites and newsletters for key terms like "catalog acquisition", "publishing deal", and "artist signed".

2. The data is cleaned, structured and deduplicated to create a single, reliable feed.

3. The results are displayed here, filterable by date, label, artist, and deal type.
